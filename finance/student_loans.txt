Federal Student Loan Guidance

Types of Federal Student Loans:
- Direct Subsidized Loans: For undergraduate students with financial need
- Direct Unsubsidized Loans: For undergraduate and graduate students, no financial need required
- Direct PLUS Loans: For graduate students and parents of dependent undergraduates
- Direct Consolidation Loans: Combine multiple federal loans into one

Interest Rates (as of 2024):
- Undergraduate Direct Loans: 5.50%
- Graduate Direct Unsubsidized Loans: 7.05%
- Direct PLUS Loans: 8.05%

Repayment Plans:
1. Standard Repayment: Fixed payments over 10 years
2. Graduated Repayment: Payments start low and increase over time (10 years)
3. Extended Repayment: Fixed or graduated payments over 25 years (must have $30,000+ in loans)
4. Income-Driven Repayment (IDR): Payments based on income and family size
   - Income-Based Repayment (IBR)
   - Pay As You Earn (PAYE)
   - Revised Pay As You Earn (REPAYE)
   - Income-Contingent Repayment (ICR)

Loan Forgiveness Programs:
- Public Service Loan Forgiveness (PSLF): Forgiveness after 120 qualifying payments for non-profit/government employees
- Teacher Loan Forgiveness: Up to $17,500 for teachers in low-income schools
- Income-Driven Repayment Forgiveness: Remaining balance forgiven after 20-25 years
- Total and Permanent Disability Discharge: For borrowers with disabilities

Deferment and Forbearance:
- Deferment: Temporary postponement of payments (may be subsidized)
- Forbearance: Temporary reduction or postponement of payments (interest continues to accrue)

When to Consider Paying Off Early:
- Interest rate is above 7%
- You have sufficient emergency savings (3-6 months of expenses)
- You're not eligible for loan forgiveness
- You have no higher-interest debt

When to Consider IDR Instead:
- Your income is low relative to your debt
- You're pursuing Public Service Loan Forgiveness
- You expect significant income increase in the future
- You need lower monthly payments for cash flow

Refinancing Considerations:
- Federal to Private: Lose federal benefits (IDR, forgiveness, deferment)
- Only refinance if you can get significantly lower rate
- Consider your credit score and income stability
