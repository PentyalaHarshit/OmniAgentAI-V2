Mortgage and Home Buying Guidance

Mortgage Types:
- Fixed-Rate Mortgage: Interest rate stays same for entire term (15, 20, 30 years)
- Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM): Rate changes periodically after initial fixed period
- FHA Loan: Lower down payment, government-backed, mortgage insurance required
- VA Loan: For veterans, no down payment, no PMI
- Jumbo Loan: For loans exceeding conforming limits

When to Pay Off Mortgage Early:
- Interest rate is high (above 6-7%)
- No higher-interest debt
- Emergency fund is fully funded
- Retirement contributions are maximized
- Close to retirement and want to be debt-free

When NOT to Pay Off Mortgage Early:
- Interest rate is low (below 4-5%)
- Could earn higher returns by investing
- Employer 401(k) match not maximized
- Emergency fund not fully funded
- Tax deduction benefit is significant

Mortgage Interest Deduction:
- Deduct interest on up to $750,000 of mortgage debt
- Must itemize deductions to benefit
- Benefit decreases as you pay down principal
- Consider after-tax cost of mortgage

Refinancing Considerations:
- Rule of thumb: Refinance if new rate is 1%+ lower than current rate
- Consider closing costs (2-5% of loan amount)
- Calculate break-even point (closing costs / monthly savings)
- Consider how long you plan to stay in home
- Check current credit score

Down Payment Guidelines:
- Traditional: 20% to avoid PMI
- FHA: 3.5% minimum
- VA: 0% down payment
- Conventional: 3-5% minimum (with PMI)

Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI):
- Required when down payment < 20%
- Typically 0.5-1% of loan amount annually
- Can be removed once equity reaches 20%
- FHA has MIP (Mortgage Insurance Premium) for life of loan

Home Buying Costs Beyond Down Payment:
- Closing costs: 2-5% of home price
- Inspection fees: $300-500
- Appraisal fee: $300-500
- Moving costs: $500-2,000+
- Initial repairs/improvements: Variable

Rent vs Buy Considerations:
- Buy if: Plan to stay 5+ years, stable income, want to build equity
- Rent if: Plan to move soon, uncertain income, prefer flexibility
- Calculate: Compare total cost of ownership vs rent including opportunity cost

Home Equity Considerations:
- Home equity line of credit (HELOC): Access equity as needed
- Home equity loan: Lump sum, fixed rate
- Risk: Home is collateral, could lose home in foreclosure
- Use for: Home improvements, education, emergency (not discretionary spending)
