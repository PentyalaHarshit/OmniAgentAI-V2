Investing and Retirement Guidance

Investment Priority Order:
1. Employer 401(k) Match (if available) - Guaranteed 100% return
2. High-Interest Debt (above 7-8%) - Guaranteed return equal to interest rate
3. Emergency Fund (3-6 months expenses) - Financial security
4. Roth IRA (if eligible) - Tax-free growth
5. Traditional 401(k)/IRA - Tax-deferred growth
6. HSA (if available) - Triple tax advantage
7. Taxable Brokerage Account - Flexible investing

Expected Returns (Historical Averages):
- Stocks (S&P 500): 10% nominal, 7% real (inflation-adjusted)
- Bonds: 4-6% nominal
- Real Estate: 3-5% (plus rental income)
- High-Yield Savings: 4-5% (varies with rates)

Asset Allocation by Age:
- 20s: 80-90% stocks, 10-20% bonds
- 30s: 70-80% stocks, 20-30% bonds
- 40s: 60-70% stocks, 30-40% bonds
- 50s: 50-60% stocks, 40-50% bonds
- 60s: 40-50% stocks, 50-60% bonds
- 70s+: 30-40% stocks, 60-70% bonds

Investment Vehicles:
- 401(k): Employer-sponsored, pre-tax contributions, employer match
- Roth IRA: After-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals, income limits
- Traditional IRA: Pre-tax contributions, taxed withdrawals
- HSA: Triple tax advantage (pre-tax, tax-free growth, tax-free withdrawals for medical)
- 529 Plan: Education savings, state tax benefits
- Taxable Brokerage: No contribution limits, no tax advantages

When to Pay Debt vs Invest:
- Pay debt first if: Interest rate > 7%, debt causes stress, variable rate debt
- Invest first if: Interest rate < 4%, employer match available, tax-advantaged space
- Split if: Interest rate 4-7%, comfortable with both, want balance

Risk Tolerance Assessment:
- Conservative: Focus on capital preservation, lower returns
- Moderate: Balance growth and stability
- Aggressive: Maximize growth, accept higher volatility

Common Investment Mistakes:
- Not taking employer 401(k) match
- Timing the market
- Emotional investing (buying high, selling low)
- Lack of diversification
- High fees (expense ratios > 0.5%)
- Not rebalancing portfolio
- Panic selling during downturns

Retirement Savings Guidelines:
- By age 30: 1x annual salary
- By age 40: 3x annual salary
- By age 50: 6x annual salary
- By age 60: 8x annual salary
- By age 67: 10x annual salary

Inflation Impact:
- Historical average: 3% annually
- Impact: Reduces purchasing power over time
- Strategy: Invest in growth assets to outpace inflation
